Death By Hellfire

A Guide To The CIA's Not-So-Secret Drone Program



By Zach Bergson

This is an interactive news story and application I created for my final project at General Assembly's WDI Program. It features an interactive map and search function that allows users to save information, animated charts created with D3.js and a live Twitter feed about drones. All drone strike data comes from the Dronestream API, which was created by The Intercept's Josh Begley.

***

Since 2002, the Central Intelligance Agency and the Joint Special Operations Command have conducted an unofficial drone-assasination program that has killed approximately 5,200 people in Pakistan, Yemen and Somalia.

George W. Bush was the first Commander-In-Chief to sign off on a targeted killing by drone strike. Fifty-three assasinations -- primarily in Pakistan -- occurred during his presidency.

By the time President Barack Obama was elected in 2008, Americans were fed up with bloody ground wars in the Middle East. His administration needed an alternative to sacrificing thousands of American lives and billions of dollars fighting on the ground, and the CIA and JSOC's drone program was the best option.

Launch map visualization

Throughout Obama's seven years in office, his administration has carried out 554 drone strikes in Pakistan, Yemen and Somalia.

Many of those attacks killed their intended targets, and they've undoubtedly saved the lives of covert military personnel who would have otherwise had to conduct those operations on the ground.

But the civilian toll has also been steep -- so steep, in fact, that many have argued the program does more harm than good for U.S. interests. Citing an unnamed government whisteblower, the Intercept recently found that in a five-month period between Jan. 2012 and Feb. 2013, 90 percent of fatalities were not intended targets.

There isn't much data available to the public on who is actually killed in each attack. But if that percentage is anywhere close to being representative of the effectiveness of the drone-assasination program as a whole, the U.S. government has killed thousands of innocent people in Pakistan, Yemen and Somalia.

Launch drone data visualizations

According to Dronestream's data (which live updates), 4,519 people have been killed by the drone-assasination program during Obama's administration alone.

Pakistan, specifically Northwest Pakistan, has felt the brunt of the drone program.

More than 3,900 people have been killed there since 2002, more than Somalia and Yemen combined. In Datta Khel, a town located near the Afghan border, 334 fatalities have occurred, a higher death toll than the entire state of Somalia.

Northwest Pakistan has been a haven for Taliban fighters from Afghanistan for decades, and many U.S. drone strikes carried out there have killed significant Taliban officials.

But many innocent civilians have been caught in the crossfire.

In 2011, for instance, a targeted attack in Datta Khel killed 40 tribal elders, tradesmen and police officers.

Beyond gratitous killings of civilians, drone assasinations have done great damage to U.S. interests in the Middle East in some cases.

In 2011, the CIA and JSOC ramped up their attacks in Yemen, and managed kill many Al-Qaeda terrorists hiding there.

But the attacks, 144 in total, also influenced Yemenis to oust former president and U.S-ally Ali Abdullah Saleh, and pave the way for the civil war that's currently ravaging the nation.

Fighting terrorism across the globe clearly isn't a black and white affair. This interactive news story can help you stay informed on a key component in America's ongoing war against terrorism across the globe.
